What is B.Sc Nursing?

B.Sc Nursing (Bachelor of Science in Nursing) is a four-year undergraduate program that trains students in nursing theory, patient care, and clinical practice through hospital postings. It is recognised by nursing councils across India and forms the foundation for careers as a staff nurse, ward supervisor, or — with further study — a nurse educator or specialist.

For many students in Kerala and neighbouring states, B.Sc Nursing admission is also the first step toward an international nursing career, since the degree is widely accepted for licensing exams abroad.

After B.Sc Nursing: careers and further study

B.Sc Nursing graduates typically begin as staff nurses in hospitals, with clear progression paths into specialised wards, nursing administration, nurse education, or postgraduate study through M.Sc Nursing. Many of our graduates also go on to sit for OET or similar licensing exams and move into hospital roles in the UK, Germany, Australia, and the Gulf — a pathway we support directly through our study and work abroad guidance.
